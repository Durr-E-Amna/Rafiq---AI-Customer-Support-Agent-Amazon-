# Damaged, defective, or wrong item received

This is treated differently from a standard "changed my mind" return, because the issue is on our end (or the seller's), not the customer's preference.

## What counts as this category

- The box arrived physically damaged, or the product inside is broken/defective
- The item received doesn't match what was ordered (wrong color, wrong model, wrong product entirely)
- The item is missing parts, accessories, or components that were supposed to be included
- A third-party seller refuses a valid return, charges an unreasonable fee, or doesn't respond — this falls under the **A-to-Z Guarantee**, not standard return policy

## What's different about this case

- These returns aren't held to the usual "must be unused, original packaging" condition in the same way, since the customer isn't returning something they simply don't want — photos of the damage/mismatch are typically requested as evidence instead
- Customers can choose either a refund or a replacement
- Even non-returnable or Final Sale items become eligible for resolution if they arrive damaged, defective, or materially different from the listing — this exception always applies

## Why this should usually route to escalation, not a generic answer

Claims of "wrong item" or "damaged on arrival" carry a meaningfully higher risk of being either a genuine fulfillment error or, occasionally, a fraudulent claim. Both directions need a human or a verified photo-based review to confirm — an AI agent should not unilaterally approve a refund or replacement based on the customer's text description alone. The agent's job here is to acknowledge the issue, explain that photo evidence will be requested, and hand off, not to make a promise about the outcome.

## A-to-Z Guarantee claims specifically

When the issue is with a third-party seller rather than a directly-fulfilled order, and the seller hasn't resolved it (no response within a reasonable window, or an unfair denial), the customer is protected by the A-to-Z Guarantee — Amazon can step in, refund the customer directly, and recover the cost from the seller separately. This always needs to be escalated as a formal claim rather than resolved as a normal refund conversation.

## Repeated complaints from the same order

If a customer mentions this is the second or third time they've had an issue with the same order, or that they've already contacted support before about it, this is a strong signal to escalate immediately rather than re-explain the policy — repeating policy at someone who's already frustrated and has been through this before tends to make things worse, not better.
