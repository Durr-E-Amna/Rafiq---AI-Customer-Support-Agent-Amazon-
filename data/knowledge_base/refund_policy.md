# Refund policy and timelines

Refunds are only issued after a returned item has been received and inspected. The clock on refund processing starts from the moment that inspection is completed — not from when the item was shipped back.

## What gets refunded

- The full price paid for the item, refunded to the original payment method
- Original shipping fees, if a guaranteed delivery date was missed (covered separately under delivery guarantees)
- Note: promotional discounts or coupon value are not refunded as separate cash — only the actual amount paid is returned.

## Refund timelines by method

| Refund destination | Time to receive refund |
|---|---|
| Amazon gift card balance | Within 2-3 hours of approval |
| Credit card | 3-5 business days (bank processing time, varies by issuer) |
| Debit card | Up to 10 business days |

Refunds always return to whichever method was actually charged — a card refund goes back to that card, a gift-card-balance purchase is refunded as gift card balance. There's no option to redirect a refund to a different method than the one originally used.

## Replacements instead of refunds

For some return reasons (item arrived damaged or defective, or doesn't match the listing), the customer can choose a replacement instead of a refund, sent out once the original is confirmed in transit back or based on the photo evidence provided.

## If a refund seems delayed

Before escalating, check:
1. Has the returned item actually been received and inspected yet? Refund timers don't start until then — typically allow up to several business days after the carrier shows delivery.
2. For card refunds, has it been fewer than 10 business days? This is normal bank processing time, not a system failure.
3. For gift card balance refunds, is the balance visible in the account? It should appear within a few hours of approval.

If all of the above check out and the refund is still missing past the stated window, this should be escalated to a human agent rather than guessed at — refund/payment discrepancies need account-level investigation.
